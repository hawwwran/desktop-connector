## Desktop Connector — Visual Identity Guide

## Purpose

This document defines a practical visual identity for **Desktop Connector** based on the current exploration direction:

- strong blue-driven technical branding,
- clean device-connection symbolism,
- geometric / abstract background language,
- and a balance between trustworthy utility software and visually distinctive branding.

This guide is meant to keep future assets visually consistent across:

- app icons,
- splash screens,
- website/repository graphics,
- social thumbnails,
- presentation backgrounds,
- Android/Desktop UI accents,
- and future marketing or documentation visuals.

It is intentionally practical rather than theoretical.

---

## Brand character

Desktop Connector should visually communicate these qualities:

- **technical**
- **trustworthy**
- **modern**
- **connected**
- **fast**
- **clean**
- **cross-device**
- **privacy-aware**
- **utility-first, not playful**

The brand should feel like a serious tool, not a toy app.

### Not the goal
The identity should **not** feel:

- childish,
- overly corporate,
- generic SaaS,
- retro,
- or heavily decorative.

It should stay visually memorable, but still look like software meant to be used daily.

---

## Core visual concept

The visual language of Desktop Connector is built from three core ideas:

### 1. Device connection
The main symbolic concept is the relationship between:

- a desktop device,
- and a phone.

This is the foundation of the brand.

### 2. Transfer / sync / movement
This is represented visually through:

- orbital arcs,
- swooshes,
- curved bands,
- and directional motion.

The visual should suggest:
- transfer,
- linking,
- synchronization,
- communication,
- and flow.

### 3. Structured digital energy
The background and supporting graphics should feel energetic, but still controlled.

This is best expressed through:

- geometric planes,
- curved layered shapes,
- blue light gradients,
- and limited warm accent contrast.

## Neutral light

### Soft white
`#E8EEFD`

---

## Recommended balance

The visual system should roughly follow this ratio:

- **70% blues**
- **20% light blues / soft neutrals**
- **10% warm accents**

That keeps the brand firmly technical and avoids drifting into a decorative or gaming-style look.

## Logo direction

## Main symbolic logo concept

The strongest current logo direction is:

- desktop monitor
- smartphone
- orbital connecting arc / swoosh
- optionally one star / spark accent
- no text in master icon version

This works because it communicates:
- desktop ↔ phone
- connection
- transfer
- movement

all at once.

---

## Logo style requirements

The logo should be:

- clean
- centered
- balanced
- readable at small sizes
- recognizable without text
- visually simple enough to become an app icon
- strong enough to work on dark or graphic backgrounds

### The logo should not be:
- overly detailed
- highly realistic
- full of tiny UI details
- visually noisy
- dependent on text to make sense

---

## Device shape guidance

### Desktop monitor
Preferred shape:
- clean rectangular monitor
- simple lower bezel
- no unnecessary circular “microphone-like” detail on the bottom
- minimal stand

### Smartphone
Preferred shape:
- simple modern slab form
- minimal notch or speaker detail if needed
- no excessive realism
- no tiny buttons or camera details unless extremely subtle

---

## Orbital connector guidance

The connection arc is a key brand device.

It should feel like:
- transfer
- motion
- linking
- directional energy

Best characteristics:
- smooth
- curved
- slightly dynamic
- not too thick
- not too decorative

It can pass:
- behind one device
- in front of the other
- or wrap around both

That layered behavior helps the symbol feel connected and alive.

---

## Symbolic / simplified variant

For small usage or simpler brand contexts, a reduced symbolic logo is recommended.

This version should be:

- flatter
- simpler
- more abstract
- less detailed
- based on essential shapes only

The symbolic version should keep only:
- monitor
- phone
- one connecting arc

Everything else becomes optional.

## Background language

## Preferred background style

The strongest current background language is:

- abstract geometric composition
- large intersecting planes
- curved overlapping bands
- blue-dominant palette
- selective yellow / orange accents
- no literal texture-heavy space scene unless intentionally desired

This style feels:
- modern
- energetic
- premium
- structured
- distinctive

---

## Background types

### Type A — pure blue technical background
Use when you need:
- cleaner UI support
- calmer composition
- more professional / restrained presentation

Characteristics:
- blue gradient structure
- light glows
- subtle layered geometry
- little or no warm accent

### Type B — geometric blue-yellow background
Use when you need:
- stronger branding
- visual energy
- expressive banners or covers
- stronger identity in presentations or repo graphics

Characteristics:
- strong blue planes
- bright yellow intersections
- layered motion
- bolder composition

### Type C — symbolic flat background
Use when you need:
- simpler graphic design
- icon backplates
- presentation section dividers
- minimal brand visuals

Characteristics:
- flatter
- simpler
- fewer layers
- more restrained contrast

---

## Background rules

### Should do
- fill the entire composition edge-to-edge
- feel intentional and balanced
- use large shape relationships
- maintain strong blue identity
- keep warm accents limited and meaningful

### Should not do
- fade into black corners
- use muddy grey transitions
- look accidental or random
- feel chaotic
- overpower the logo or UI content placed on top

---

## Composition rules

## Centering
When used as master artwork or app image:
- the main symbol should be centered
- the layout should feel optically centered, not just mathematically centered

## Breathing room
The main symbol should not be too cramped.
Leave enough negative space to keep it readable.

## Scale
Use three rough scale modes:

### Large symbol mode
For icon exploration and hero graphics:
- the symbol dominates
- background supports it

### Balanced mode
For branding panels and banners:
- symbol and background share attention

### Background-only mode
For UI backplates and banners:
- no device symbol
- only abstract brand geometry

---

## Shape language

The system should combine two families of shapes:

### 1. Device geometry
- straight edges
- rectangles
- rounded corners
- clean outlines

This represents structure and technology.

### 2. Motion geometry
- arcs
- ribbons
- orbital curves
- intersecting planes

This represents connection and transfer.

The combination of these two is the core visual identity.

## App icon guidance

## Master icon artwork
The master icon artwork should:
- be created on a full square canvas
- not already fake a rounded-square app icon container
- fill the whole image naturally
- remain usable for later cropping/masking by platform rules

## App icon simplification
For actual launcher icon use, simplify aggressively:
- reduce background complexity
- keep one primary symbol
- ensure strong readability at small size

The launcher icon should feel:
- instantly recognizable
- not busy
- not text-dependent

---

## Text usage

### Brand name treatment
If text is used, it should be:
- clean sans-serif
- modern
- bold or semi-bold
- not playful
- not futuristic in a cliché sci-fi way

### Preferred text colors
On dark backgrounds:
- `#E8EEFD`
- `#A4D0FB`

For limited emphasis:
- `#3986FC`
- `#FDD00C` very sparingly

### Text should not be:
- overly glowing
- decorative
- outlined heavily
- gradient-heavy unless in specific hero artwork

---

## UI styling guidance

The visual identity should influence the UI through:

- dark blue anchors
- vivid blue active states
- light blue highlights
- occasional yellow emphasis
- clean spacing
- restrained use of effects

### Good UI translation
- buttons in `#2058F0`
- hover/focus in `#3986FC`
- strong dark surfaces in `#0920AC`
- accent highlights in `#FDD00C`
- text on dark surfaces in `#E8EEFD`

### Bad UI translation
- too many gradients everywhere
- too much yellow in functional UI
- too much decoration
- strong background artwork behind important text areas

---

## Do / Don’t summary

## Do
- keep the brand blue-led
- make the connection symbol central
- use clean device silhouettes
- use curved motion for transfer symbolism
- use geometric backgrounds intentionally
- simplify where needed
- preserve readability at small scale

## Don’t
- let warm colors dominate
- over-detail the monitor or phone
- rely on text in icons
- use muddy dark fades
- make the identity cartoonish
- turn everything into glowing effects
- use random abstract backgrounds unrelated to the brand language
